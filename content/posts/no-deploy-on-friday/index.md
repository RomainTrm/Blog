If you're working in IT, you've probably already encountered the "No deploy on friday" mantra/dogma. It's so ubiquitous that they're many about it on the internet.

![Several meme about "No deploy on friday"](1.png)

Recently, one of my LinkedIn relation commented a post that way saying something like : "If you had to deploy today (friday), I'm sorry for you". In his comment, he argued that he had shiped more than 10 versions that day and it was no big deal for him. As I was reading to this discussion, I realized they were both right, and above all, the way they were positioning about "no deploy on friday" said a lot about their business and organizational contextes as well as their technical practices.

- no deploy on friday
  - numberous meme
  - true rule some people
  - false myth for others
  - lot of cargo cult
- Goal: avoid breaking the software before the weekend (when the team is unavailable)
- I realized the way we use this mantra says a lot about us and our context
- Business
  - impact of a broken software during the weekend ?
- Organizational
  - how often we deploy ?
  - is deployment an event ?
- Technical
  - ability for 0 downtime
  - feature toggles
